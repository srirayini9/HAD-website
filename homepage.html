Hypertension

Hypertension, also known as high blood pressure, occurs when the force of the blood against the artery walls is consistently too high. Over time, this can lead to health problems such as heart disease and stroke.

Causes are linked to genetics, age, and environmental factors.

Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes: An autoimmune condition where the body’s immune system attacks the insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. It typically develops in childhood or early adulthood. Type 2 Diabetes: The most common form of diabetes, where the body either becomes resistant to insulin or doesn’t produce enough. Often related to lifestyle factors and more common in adults.

Causes are linked to genetics and other lifestyle factors.

Ways to prevent hypertension and diabetes

Healthy Diet

Eat more fruits, vegetables, and whole grains; limit salt and sugar to control blood pressure and sugar levels.

Exercise

Regular physical activity helps lower blood pressure and improves blood sugar control.

Healthy Weight

Maintaining or losing weight reduces the risk of high blood pressure and diabetes.
